Preparation for Astronomy Selection

Solving scientific problems with precision

I have been preparing for astronomy selection by studying spherical astronomy, Kepler’s laws, and Newtonian mechanics. These topics strengthen both calculation skills and conceptual understanding of celestial motions.

  • • Practice with coordinate systems and celestial positions
  • • Understanding orbital motion and planetary behavior
  • • Building confidence in physics and mathematics problem-solving

Astronomy Observation Record

Tracking the sky with discipline

My observation notes include the Galilean moons of Jupiter, the use of an equatorial mount telescope setup, and records of deep-sky objects seen through the night sky.

  • • Followed moon and planet movement over several nights
  • • Learned the importance of alignment and tracking
  • • Built a habit of careful observation and documentation

Science Project

Testing thermal behavior of materials

My scientific project explored how different materials behave under heat and how controlled testing can reveal useful information about durability, conductivity, and physical change.

  • • Applied the scientific method for accurate testing
  • • Compared results across multiple conditions
  • • Connected laboratory practice to real-world science
